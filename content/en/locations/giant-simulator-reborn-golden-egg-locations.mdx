Finding every egg requires keen observation, as many spawns are tucked away in hidden nooks, underwater caverns, or high-altitude structures. Memorizing this layout will allow you to complete your quests in under five minutes per run.

### 1. Stonehenge Ruins
This is the most straightforward spawn point on the map. Located in the open plains biome, Stonehenge consists of a circular arrangement of massive stone pillars. The Golden Egg spawns directly in the center of this stone circle. Because it is highly visible from almost anywhere in the plains, this egg is heavily contested by other giants.

### 2. The Waterfall Pool
Near the majestic waterfall that feeds the map's river, you will find a prime spawning ground. The egg typically rests on the grassy banks right in front of the cascading water, or occasionally submerged in the shallow edges of the pool. It is an easy grab on your way to more hidden locations.

### 3. The Underwater Cave (The Ultimate Secret)
This is the hardest spawn to find if you do not know the trick. Head to the deep plunge pool directly beneath the waterfall. You must shrink your character size to 5% (the minimum limit) to prevent bouncing off the terrain. Dive underwater in first-person view, swim toward the far-left corner near a large boulder, and you will discover a submerged tunnel. Follow this cave system to emerge into a secret cavern containing a guaranteed Golden Egg spawn and a mysterious locked temple door.

### 4. The Temple Roof
For players who enjoy parkour, the roof of the main temple/castle structure holds a regular spawn. To reach it, scale the side walls or use nearby hills to launch your character. Maintaining a medium size (around 50%) is recommended here; if you are too small, you lack the jump height, but if you are too large, your character's physics will slide off the curved roof tiles.

### 5. The Volcano Cave
Beneath the towering volcano lies a dark, rocky cavern. Enter the cave entrance located near the base of the volcano. The Golden Egg spawns in the shadowy corners near the path leading toward the Temple portal. The ambient red glow of the lava makes the amber silhouette of the egg slightly harder to spot, so look closely.

### 6. The Desert Sand Dunes
In the arid desert biome, eggs spawn in the depressions between the massive sand dunes. Specifically, check the far-middle dune area. Because the hills block your line of sight, you must actively climb over the crests of the dunes to spot the egg resting in the sandy nooks below.
