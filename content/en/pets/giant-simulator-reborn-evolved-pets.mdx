<Section icon={Swords} title="How to Evolve Your Pets Step-by-Step">

Evolving your companions is a deterministic system that rewards active play and strategic planning. Unlike standard pet hatching, which relies entirely on luck, evolution allows you to turn common duplicates into high-tier powerhouses. The fundamental rule of pet evolution in Giant Simulator Reborn is simple: you must fuse three identical copies of a pet to unlock its evolved form.

This fusion process permanently consumes the three duplicates but rewards you with a single companion that boasts doubled base stats and enhanced passive abilities. Follow this step-by-step progression route to successfully evolve your first companion.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Accumulate Gold or Event Shells">
    Before you can evolve pets, you need the currency to hatch them. Redeem active codes like 'Demonking' and 'Pets' to secure an easy 50,001 Gold starter fund, or farm the Summer Update island for event Shells.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Purchase Multiple Pet Chests">
    Head to the pet shop or the event portal and purchase pet chests in bulk. Keep opening chests until you have collected at least three copies of the exact same companion.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Access the Evolution Menu">
    Open your pet inventory screen. Select one of your duplicate pets and click the 'Evolve' button to open the fusion interface. The game will automatically detect if you have the required duplicates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Confirm Fusion and Lock the Pet">
    Confirm the evolution to merge the three duplicates into a single evolved pet. Immediately click the padlock icon on your new companion to lock it, preventing accidental deletion during future mass-selling sessions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Anti-Loss Warning">
Never use the mass-sell or auto-sell features without locking your evolved companions first. If you accidentally sell an evolved pet, the game does not offer a recovery option, and your hard-earned evolution progress will be permanently lost.
